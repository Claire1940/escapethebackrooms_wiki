Level 1, officially known as **The Habitable Zone**, is a critical early stage in *Escape the Backrooms*. Players often encounter this level as a sprawling, concrete **parking garage**, a familiar yet unsettling environment. This area introduces several core mechanics, including sanity drain and entity encounters, making it a pivotal challenge for new players. Understanding its layout and objectives is crucial for successful progression.

The level is characterized by its concrete structure, often featuring office rooms and scattered resources like Almond Water. As you explore, remember that your sanity will begin to deplete here, adding a new layer of psychological horror to the experience.

<Callout type="tip" title="Sanity Management">
Always prioritize collecting **Almond Water** on Level 1. Your sanity will constantly drain, and running out can lead to detrimental effects or even death. Keep an eye on your sanity meter and use Almond Water strategically.

<Section icon={Target} title="Objectives and Progression through the Parking Garage">
Successfully escaping the **Escape the Backrooms garage** requires completing a series of interconnected objectives across its multiple floors. Each floor presents unique challenges and puzzles that must be overcome to progress.

### Part 1: Unlock the Elevator (Floor 1)

Upon spawning, your first task is to find the **color-coded lock** on an elevator that leads to Floor 2. The code for this lock is hidden within a red door leading to a room containing four distinctively colored cars.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Red Door">
    Explore Floor 1 until you find a **red door**. This door leads to the car room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify Car Colors">
    Inside the room, observe the colors of the four cars. For example, if you see one blue, two red, and one green car, this sequence forms your code.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Input the Code">
    Return to the elevator and input the color sequence into the **color-coded lock**. Each colored car corresponds to a button on the lock.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Don't Rush">
While exploring Floor 1, make sure to search office rooms for essential items like **Almond Water** and **Flashlights**. These will be vital for later sections.
</Callout>

### Part 2: Get the Keys (Floor 2)

Floor 2 is significantly larger than Floor 1. Your goal here is to collect four keys scattered throughout the area to open a vault door to Floor 3.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Key Locations">
    - Desks
    - Closets
    - Open drawers
    <br />
    **Note**: Always check all accessible furniture.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Threats on Floor 2">
    - **Skin-Stealer**: One in single-player, two in multiplayer (3+ players).
    - **Strategy**: Maintain situational awareness and use stealth or speed to avoid.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Once all four keys are acquired, locate the room with the **vault door**. This door requires simultaneous key activation.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Find the Vault Door">
    Search Floor 2 for a room containing a large vault door.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Insert Keys into Keyholes">
    Place all four keys into their respective keyholes around the room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Simultaneous Turn">
    Quickly turn all four keys at the same time. They will unturn if not activated fast enough. Success opens the vault to Floor 3.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Part 3: Find the Elevator (Floor 3)

Floor 3 is a dimly lit area composed of three individual sub-floors. While each sub-floor has a slightly different layout, the objective remains the same: find the elevator to Floor 4.

<Callout type="info" title="Resource Scarcity">
Items are scarce on Floor 3, primarily found at the start. Manage your **Flashlight** battery and **Almond Water** carefully. A **Skin-Stealer** also roams this floor.
</Callout>

### Part 4: Unlock the Gate (Floor 4)

Floor 4 is a small, rough concrete room with a closed gate. To escape, you need to override the security.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate The Hub Door">
    Find The Hub Door, typically on the left side of the room.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access The Hub">
    Enter The Hub and use the computers in The M.E.G. Base to gain access to the gate's switch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Return and Activate">
    Go back to Floor 4 and flip the switch to open the gate.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Part 5: STAY iN THE LiGHT (Floor 4 Parking Garage)

After the gate opens, you'll enter an area resembling another parking garage. This section is perilous due to **Smilers** that appear when the lights go out.

<Callout type="warning" title="Smiler Threat">
When the lights flicker off, **Smilers** will appear. Immediately run to the nearest small hallway. The lights only stay on for 5-10 seconds, so move quickly. Avoid the ramp unless you're pursuing the "Wrong Door Ending."
</Callout>

### Environmental Notes and Trivia

The developers have included several interesting details and historical facts about Level 1.

*   **Floor 4's Ambience**: Interestingly, the ambiance for Floor 4 is named "Level1_Ambience" in game files, while Floor 1's is "Level1_Ambience_2." This is likely because Floor 4 was the first part of Level 1 to be developed and added to the game.
*   **Early Development**: In early versions of the game, the garage part of Floor 4 was shown without the connecting hallways, highlighting iterative design changes.
*   **Removed Entities**: Originally, entities like Ghost Smilers were planned for Level 1 but were later removed. Other entities like Scratchers, Facelings, and Hounds were considered but never fully implemented.
