Level 0.1, aptly named "The Danger Zone," is a crucial sub-level within *Escape the Backrooms*, often accessed from Level 0.2, the Remodeled Mess. This area is characterized by its maze-like structure, locked doors, and a constant threat from various entities. Successfully navigating this level involves a combination of exploration, puzzle-solving, and quick reactions to evade lurking dangers. The primary objective is to open all necessary doors and power five electrical boxes to secure passage.

<Section icon={Target} title="Navigating the Remodeled Mess and Danger Zone">
The journey to and through the Danger Zone begins in Level 0.2, the Remodeled Mess. After completing the initial tasks in the arcade area and accessing Level 0.2, players face the formidable Bone Thief. To bypass this entity, you must descend into Level 0.1.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Accessing Level 0.1 from Remodeled Mess">
    From Level 0.2, navigate through the hallways until you find the large maze with locked doors. Look for the "no-clipping floors" – these static-like areas will transport you to Level 0.1.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Opening Doors in the Danger Zone">
    Once in Level 0.1, your main task is to open several locked doors. These doors, once opened in the Danger Zone, will also unlock their corresponding counterparts in Level 0.2. This process requires thorough exploration and often involves triggering pressure plates or switches.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Powering Electrical Boxes">
    After opening the doors, you must return to Level 0.2 via the no-clipping floors to power on five electrical boxes. This step cannot be completed in Level 0.1. Successfully powering all boxes will allow you to shut the gate and bypass the Bone Thief.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Evading Entities">
    Throughout both Level 0.2 and 0.1, you will encounter the **Bone Thief**, **Skin Stealers**, and **Smilers**. The Bone Thief will lunge and grab you if you cross its sightline, while Skin Stealers and Smilers will chase you. Utilize infinite stamina when being chased and environmental elements to your advantage.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip">
When in Level 0.1, prioritize finding and opening all doors. This makes the subsequent task of powering electrical boxes in Level 0.2 much smoother, as you won't need to re-enter the Danger Zone as frequently.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Survival Strategies for the Danger Zone">
Mastering Level 0.1 requires more than just basic evasion. Strategic thinking and understanding the game's mechanics can give you an edge.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="No-Clipping Floor Mechanics">
    The "no-clipping floors" between Level 0.2 and 0.1 appear static-like. They are not direct passages but rather warp points. Stepping on them causes you to fall out of bounds, then instantly warp to the corresponding location in the sub-level. Use this to your advantage for quick transitions, but be aware of entities on both sides.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Team Coordination (Co-op)">
    In multiplayer, designate roles. One player can focus on opening doors in Level 0.1 while others provide cover or scout ahead. Communicate entity locations and progress on electrical boxes. Splitting up can be risky but efficient if coordinated.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Flashlight Management">
    While some entities like Smilers might be deterred by light, relying solely on your flashlight can be dangerous as it can reveal your position. Use it judiciously, especially in dark areas where Smilers are prevalent.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Map Layout Awareness">
    Despite the disorienting nature, try to memorize key pathways and the location of locked doors and electrical boxes. Even small details, like the differing wall structures between 0.1 and 0.2, can be crucial for navigation.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>
