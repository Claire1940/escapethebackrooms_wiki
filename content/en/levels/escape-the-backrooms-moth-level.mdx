<Section icon={Shield} title="Survival Strategies Against Deathmoths">
Surviving Level 5 relies heavily on stealth and quick reactions. The Female Deathmoths are incredibly dangerous, but they have predictable patterns that can be exploited. Your primary goal is to avoid detection and use the environment to your advantage.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Maintain Stealth with Crouching">
    When you sense a moth is nearby (screen blur, high temperature reading if you have a thermometer), immediately **crouch**. Female Deathmoths are less likely to detect you while you are crouched. Move slowly and deliberately.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Avoid Flashlight Usage">
    Flashlights are a moth's worst enemy, and yours if you use one. **Do not shine your flashlight** at large moths in Level 5. This will instantly draw their aggression and lead to a chase. It's best to keep your flashlight off entirely in areas with big moths.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Utilize Bug Spray Valves">
    If a large moth spots you or is blocking your path, locate the nearest **bug spray valve** (often steam valves). Turning these on will release a large insecticide spray that forces the moth to retreat to its original position, clearing your way temporarily.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Deal with Small Moths">
    Level 5 also features smaller moths. These can often be killed directly with small bottles of insecticide, turning them into "Moth Jelly." While less threatening than their larger counterparts, they can still be a nuisance and potentially reveal your position.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Identify the True Exit">
    The level is designed to confuse. Many doors are fake and lead to immediate danger (hives). Always look for a door with a **green exit sign** above it. This is your only safe way out.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Temperature Readings">
A thermometer can indicate moth proximity. A reading of **125°F (51.5°C)** suggests a Female Deathmoth is nearby, while **140°F (60°C)** means a hive is very close. If you get a 140°F reading, leave the area immediately.
</Callout>
