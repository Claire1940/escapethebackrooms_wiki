<Section icon={Target} title="Part 1: The Main Hall Objectives">
Your journey in Level 5 begins in The Main Hall, a grand, albeit eerie, area with 1930s-era furniture. Your primary goal here is to gather three keys to unlock the elevator leading to The Hotel. These keys are obtained by feeding Moth Jelly to a dumbwaiter.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Equip Bug Spray & Solve Painting Puzzle">
    Upon spawning, locate the desk and equip a Bug Spray. Identify the four paintings on the walls. Click them in order from the youngest person to the oldest person depicted: a girl with two hair loops, a man in a fancy suit with a serious face, a man in a fancy suit looking directly at the viewer, and finally, a man with a wrinkled face. This sequence will open a door.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Collect Moth Jelly">
    The Main Hall is infested with Male Deathmoths. Find clusters of these moths and use your Bug Spray on them. They will die and turn into Moth Jelly. You need three portions of Moth Jelly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Use the Dumbwaiter for Keys">
    Locate the dumbwaiter down the left hallway. A note reads "_iF YOU FEED THEM THEY WiLL REWARD YOU_". Place one Moth Jelly into the dumbwaiter and send it down. It will return with a key. Repeat this process three times to obtain all three keys.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock the Elevator">
    With all three keys, return to the glass door that has three locks. Use the keys to unlock it. This will reveal a hallway leading to an elevator. Enter the elevator to proceed to The Hotel.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Moth Jelly Respawn">
If you accidentally consume a Moth Jelly, don't worry. When you kill the last remaining Male Deathmoth, another one will spawn elsewhere, allowing you to collect more.
</Callout>

</Section>

### The Mailbox Puzzle

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify Open Mailboxes">
    Upon exiting the elevator, you'll see a mailbox with numbered compartments. Seven of these boxes will be open (the specific ones vary per playthrough). These correspond to the rooms you need to visit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Collect Papers from Rooms">
    Navigate to each of the rooms indicated by the open mailboxes. Inside, you will find papers on a drawer. Collect these papers.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Return Papers to Mailbox">
    Place each collected paper into its corresponding open compartment in the mailbox. Once all papers are placed, a code will appear on the ceiling. The papers themselves spell out "LOOK UP" as a hint.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Unlock Room 235">
    Take the 5-digit code from the ceiling and input it into the number lock on Room 235. This door will then open, granting access to the stairs leading to The Boiler Room.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Possible Room 235 Codes">
There are only three possible 5-digit codes for the Room 235 lock. If you're having trouble, one of these will be correct: **17564**, **05938**, or **89472**.
</Callout>

</Section>

### Dealing with Female Deathmoths

| Evasion Tactic | Description                                                               | Effectiveness | Notes                                                            |
|----------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------------|------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Thermometer**| Shows temperature; 72°F is safe, higher indicates nearby moths.           | High          | Essential for early detection.                                   |
| **Crouching**  | Moths cannot harm you while crouching, regardless of proximity.             | Very High     | Most reliable method for direct evasion.                         |
| **Red Valves** | Pressing these releases bug spray, blocking moths from advancing.         | High          | Useful if a moth is chasing you and you can't crouch immediately.|
| **Flashlight Off**| Recommended to turn off flashlight when approaching moths to reduce visibility. | Moderate      | Best combined with crouching.                                    |
