<Section icon={Shield} title="Essential Survival Tools and Mechanics">

To survive the **escape the backrooms boiler room**, you must master the tools provided at the start of the section. Upon entering Room 235 and ascending the stairs, you will find a table equipped with Thermometers. These are not optional; they are your primary means of detecting threats before they become visible in the dark, steam-filled corridors.

  <Card title="Thermometer">
    - **Safe Temp**: 72°F indicates no immediate threats nearby.
    - **Danger Temp**: Spikes to 120°F+ mean a Female Deathmoth is in the room.
    - **Precision**: Use it to check corners before turning.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Red Valves">
    - **Defense**: Turning these releases a jet of high-pressure steam.
    - **Repellant**: Steam acts as a physical barrier that moths cannot cross.
    - **Timing**: Best used when being actively chased by an aggroed moth.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Visual Cues">

The temperature mechanic is consistent. If your thermometer reads exactly 72 degrees, you are in a "neutral" zone. However, the labyrinthine nature of the pipes means you can walk into a 120-degree zone in a matter of seconds. Observant players can also look for "shimmering" air, which serves as a visual warning of a nearby moth even if the thermometer is tucked away.

<Section icon={Target} title="Navigating the Deathmoth Labyrinth">

Navigating the **escape the backrooms boiler room** is a test of patience. The Female Deathmoths are massive, often occupying the entire ceiling or blocking narrow hallways. Unlike the small male moths in the hotel, these entities will devour your soul instantly if they detect you while you are standing or running.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Entry">
    Pick up the thermometer from the table. Check the first corridor; if it is 72 degrees, proceed forward until you reach the first junction.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The First Encounter">
    When the thermometer jumps to 120°F, immediately hold the **Control** (Crouch) key. As long as you are crouching, the giant moths generally cannot harm you, even if you walk directly under them.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Pipe Following">
    Follow the large industrial pipes along the ceiling and walls. These often lead toward the center of the level and toward the eventual exit.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Valve Defense">
    If you accidentally stand up and trigger a moth's aggression, sprint toward the nearest red valve. Interact with it to release steam, which will force the moth to retreat and reset its patrol.
  </Step>
</Steps>

The maze contains three distinct checkpoints. If you die, you will respawn at the most recent checkpoint you passed. These are usually marked by small tables or lanterns. Use these as landmarks to ensure you are progressing in the right direction rather than walking in circles.

| Entity Type | Behavior | Countermeasure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Male Deathmoth** | Defensive/Swarming | Bug Spray / Moth Jelly |
| **Female Deathmoth** | Highly Aggressive | Crouching / Steam Valves |
| **Hound/Skin-Stealer** | Roaming (Hotel only) | Hiding in Closets/Beds |

<Callout type="success" title="Crouching Mechanic">
There is a known strategy (possibly a glitch) where height doesn't matter as much as the "Crouched" state. Even if you are slightly elevated on a pipe, as long as you are holding the crouch button, the moths will often ignore you.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Entity Management and Trap Doors">

The **escape the backrooms boiler room** is famous for its "Fake Doors." The level is littered with doors that look identical to the exit, but opening the wrong one can lead to a swift death. Most of these doors lead to small rooms packed with Deathmoth Hives. Once opened, the moths inside will swarm the player, leaving very little room for escape.

The giant Female Deathmoths are the primary residents here. There are approximately nine of them scattered throughout the Boiler Room. Some are stationary, guarding specific hallways, while others have limited patrol paths. Understanding their placement is key to a successful speedrun or a safe first-time completion.

| Door Color/Type | Marker | Result |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wooden Door** | None | Dead End / Trap |

<Callout type="warning" title="Flashlight Aggro">
Giant moths are extremely sensitive to light. If you shine your flashlight directly at their heads, they will aggro regardless of whether you are crouching. Use your peripheral vision or the thermometer to navigate instead.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="The Final Path to Level 6">

The final stretch of the **escape the backrooms boiler room** requires a "run for it" mentality if your sanity is low, or a slow, methodical crawl if you have the time. The exit is located at the end of a long, straight corridor that appears after you pass the final two moth encounters. 

To find the correct path:
1. Follow the orange pipes that run along the floor in the latter half of the level.
2. Look for the landmarks—specifically the large cylindrical containers.
3. Once you see a door with a glowing green **EXIT** sign, you have reached the end. 

Opening this door will immediately transport you to **Level 6: Lights Out**, a level that focuses on total darkness and sound-based navigation. Ensure you have recovered as much sanity as possible before stepping through, as Level 6 is even more taxing on your mental state.
