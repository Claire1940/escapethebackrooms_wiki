<Section icon={Map} title="Environmental Overview: The Ocean of Thalassophobia">

**Escape the Backrooms Level 7**, aptly named Thalassophobia, presents players with a daunting, seemingly infinite ocean. Unlike the cramped hallways of previous levels, this environment is defined by its vastness and the psychological dread of deep water. The level is populated by numerous suburban houses floating or partially submerged within the dark, murky brine. The seafloor is composed of sand and jagged rock formations, which become critical landmarks during the underwater segment.

The atmosphere is heavy with the sound of creaking wood and distant water movements. Upon entering from Level 6, players find themselves in a "Starting House" that serves as the initial staging area. This level acts as a major transition point in the game's progression, moving away from industrial and office-like settings into more surreal, naturalistic horrors.

<Section icon={Target} title="Surface Navigation: Following the Light">

The first half of **Escape the Backrooms Level 7** takes place on the water's surface. After exiting the starting house, you must utilize the available boats to explore the surrounding structures. Survival here depends on resource management and recognizing when the environment begins to change.

As you move further into the ocean, a thick fog will eventually roll in, obscuring your vision and making standard navigation impossible. This is a scripted event designed to test your patience and observation. After several minutes of waiting in the fog, the lighthouse located on a distant house will ignite. This beam is your only reliable way to find the final house.

| Item | Location | Utility |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Almond Water** | Various Houses | Restores sanity and health |
| **Juice** | Kitchen Counters | Stamina boost for swimming |
| **Flare Gun** | Early Floating House | Illuminates fog and signals locations |
| **Diving Helmet** | Lighthouse Dock | Required for the Level 8 transition |

<Callout type="warning" title="Fog Hazard">
When the fog appears, do not panic and drive your boat aimlessly. Stay stationary or move slowly until the lighthouse beam cuts through the mist. Driving too far into the void can make it harder to spot the light.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="The Descent: Encountering The Thing">

Once you reach the lighthouse house, the gameplay shifts from exploration to a scripted horror sequence. You will find four diving helmets near a structure known as the Shark Tank. Putting on a helmet is mandatory to proceed. After entering the tank, it will begin to descend into the crushing depths of the ocean.

During this descent, players will encounter **The Thing on Level 7**. This entity is a massive, serpent-like creature that stalks the waters. In a scripted event, the entity will attack and shatter the Shark Tank, forcing you into the open water. This transition is one of the most intense moments in **escape the backrooms level 7**, requiring quick reflexes to navigate the debris.

| Phase | Player Action | Entity Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tank Descent** | Observe through glass | Circles the tank, builds tension |
| **The Breach** | Prepare for impact | Smashes the tank, destroying it |
| **Open Water** | Swim toward the seafloor | Roams the vicinity (Avoid direct contact) |

<Callout type="success" title="Survival Strategy">
The Thing's attack on the tank is unavoidable. Once the glass breaks, don't try to fight; focus entirely on swimming downward toward the rock structures on the ocean floor.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Underwater Navigation: Finding the Cave">

The final segment of Level 7 requires you to navigate the ocean floor. The visibility is extremely low, and the pressure of the deep water adds a layer of urgency. Your goal is to find a specific rock structure that houses the entrance to a cave. This cave is the only exit leading to Level 8.

The seafloor is littered with rocks and sand. Look for artificial light sources; the cave entrance is marked by internal lights that stand out against the blue-black darkness of the abyss. Once you enter the cave, the water level will eventually drop, or you will find a path that transitions directly into the rocky tunnels of the next level.

**Underwater Comparison:**
| Surface Level | Deep Sea Floor |
| :--- | :--- |
| High visibility (pre-fog) | Extremely low visibility |
| Boat-based movement | Swimming and crawling |
| Multiple houses/loot | Singular exit path |
| Atmospheric dread | Direct entity threat |

<Callout type="info" title="Visual Cues">
If you are struggling to find the cave, look for the brightest point on the seafloor. The developers placed lights inside the cave specifically to act as a beacon for players who are running low on oxygen or sanity.
</Callout>
