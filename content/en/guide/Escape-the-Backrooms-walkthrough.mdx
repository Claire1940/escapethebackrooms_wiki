<Section icon={Target} title="Progressing Through Advanced Levels: Hub, Office, and Hotel">
After Level 0, you'll encounter more complex environments like Level 1 (The Habitable Zone), the Hub, Abandoned Office, and Terror Hotel. These levels introduce new entities, sanity mechanics, and intricate puzzles.

<Callout type="tip" title="Sanity Management is Key">
Your sanity drains passively in most levels and rapidly in danger. **Almond Water** restores 50% sanity, while **Energy Bars** and **Juice** restore 25% and provide temporary stamina boosts. Always keep a stock!
</Callout>

### Level 1: The Habitable Zone
Find a four-digit color-based elevator code from cars in the parking lot. This code is randomized. Once entered, proceed to the second floor.

### Abandoned Office (Level 4)
This level features the "Strangler" entity, visible through windows. Avoid direct contact. A key puzzle involves counting objects in a room (water coolers, books, chairs, tables) and inputting the numbers into vending machines.

### Terror Hotel (Level 5)
The Terror Hotel is split into multiple sections.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Lobby Puzzle">
    Solve a painting puzzle by pressing buttons in ascending order of the subjects' age. Collect **Bug Spray** for later use.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Moth Jelly Collection">
    Use Bug Spray on male Death Moths to obtain **Moth Jelly**, which can be fed to a dumbwaiter to get keys.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Hotel Rooms Challenge">
    Collect letters from nightstands in various rooms while avoiding **Skin Stealers** (left side) and **Hounds** (right side). The letters spell out a code (e.g., 17564, 89472, 05938) for the exit door.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Boiler Room Maze">
    Navigate a maze filled with female Death Moths. Use a **thermometer** to detect them and crouch to avoid detection. Turn red valves to scare them off if chased.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Co-op Strategy for Terror Hotel">
In co-op, one player can bait a Skin Stealer while others collect keys. Remember, Skin Stealers cannot open doors, so use this to your advantage by closing doors behind you.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Confronting Entities and Environmental Hazards">
Many levels in **Escape the Backrooms** are designed around specific entities and environmental challenges. Understanding their behaviors is crucial for survival.

<Callout type="info" title="Entity Encounter Guide">
- **Smilers**: Avoid direct light from flickering zones. Run and jump to conserve stamina.
- **Hounds**: Can be stunned with a flashlight. Close doors behind you to deter them.
- **Skin Stealers**: Mimic wanderers. Check ID cards in co-op. Close doors to slow them down.
- **Wretch**: Blind, but highly sensitive to sound. Crouch to avoid detection in dark areas.
- **Partygoers**: Avoid getting too close; they will chase you. Hide under tables.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Solving Complex Puzzles and Level Mechanics">
**Escape the Backrooms** is filled with unique puzzles that require keen observation and logical thinking. From color codes to object counting, each level presents a distinct challenge.

### Level 0.2: Residential Area (No-Clipping)
This level requires you to activate 5 switches scattered in smaller rooms. The rooms are locked from the safe side, so you must **no-clip** into "The Red Rooms" below. Traffic lights indicate danger levels (red/yellow for danger, green for safe).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Identify No-Clip Zones">
    Locate areas marked with yellow tape that allow you to no-clip into the Red Rooms.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Traffic Lights">
    Before jumping, check the traffic lights. Green means safe, red/yellow means danger (Smilers and Skin Stealers present).
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Open Doors Below">
    Once in the Red Rooms, find and open the corresponding doors from below.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Activate Breaker Boxes">
    No-clip back to the safe side and activate the electrical boxes by clicking when the arrow is in the green zone. Repeat for all 5 switches.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Sprint to the Elevator">
    After activating all switches, a gate will lower, temporarily blocking a Bone Thief. Sprint across to the elevator.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="No-Clip Disorientation">
No-clipping is not always 1:1; your position may shift. The layout of the Red Rooms differs from the safe side, so memorizing above-ground landmarks won't help below.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Achieving All Endings and Nightmare Mode">
**Escape the Backrooms** features multiple endings, each unlocking unique achievements. Completing them all, including the daunting Nightmare mode, is the ultimate goal for 100% completion.

<Callout type="tip" title="Nightmare Mode Preparation">
In Nightmare difficulty, a single death means restarting the entire game. Master all levels in normal mode first, understand entity patterns, and utilize item duplication glitches if available to maximize your chances.
</Callout>

### Understanding the Endings
There are five distinct endings to discover:

1.  **The Wrong Door Ending (Ending A)**: Typically the first ending encountered. After completing Level 55.1 (the photo collection level), you'll proceed through a door that leads to this ending.
2.  **The Pink House Ending (Ending B)**: Located in Level 974 (Kitty's House). This ending involves collecting 12 teddy bears while being pursued by the entity "Kitty," who can only be deterred by constant eye contact.
3.  **The Suburbs Ending (Ending C)**: Accessed via Level 922 (Infinite Staircase). Instead of climbing to the top, you must jump down from floor 94 or higher.
4.  **The Empty City Ending (Ending D)**: Found after completing the "Overgrowth" and "Grassrooms" levels. This involves guiding wanderers down slides and navigating a knife-based puzzle.
5.  **The True Ending**: Achieved by unlocking all previous endings (A, B, C, and D). Once all are complete, return to Level 3999 (Arcade) and interact with the final arcade machine.

<Callout type="success" title="Exploiting Nightmare Mode">
For Nightmare runs, consider using glitches like freezing entities in Level 1 or skipping sections (e.g., the cave section by climbing out of bounds) to streamline your progress. Item duplication can also alleviate sanity drain challenges.
</Callout>
