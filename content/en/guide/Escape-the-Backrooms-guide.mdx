<Section icon={Shield} title="Surviving Diverse Threats: Smilers, Hounds, and Moths">
As you delve deeper into the Backrooms, the variety and danger of entities increase. Each level presents unique challenges and requires specific strategies to survive. Understanding each entity's behavior is paramount.

<Callout type="warning">
Sanity decreases rapidly in danger. Always be aware of your surroundings and have Almond Water ready to restore it.
</Callout>

The Hub acts as a central point, allowing access to various levels. Upon entering the M.E.G. Base from the Hub, you'll solve a puzzle to open a gate. Once back in the Habitable Zone, you'll encounter **Smilers** in dimly lit rooms. These entities thrive in darkness, so staying in illuminated areas is critical.

| Entity Type | Levels Encountered | Behavior | Countermeasures |
|-------------|--------------------|----------|-----------------|
| **Smilers** | Habitable Zone, Pipe Dreams | Appear in dark, disappear in light | Stay in light, run during chases |
| **Hounds**  | Electrical Station, Terror Hotel | Chase on sight | Stun with flashlight (3 stuns on normal), then run |
| **Death Moths** (Male) | Terror Hotel | Stationary in groups | Spray with bug spray |
| **Death Moths** (Female) | Terror Hotel | Motion sensitive, high heat signature | Crouch, sneak, run if spotted, use steam valves |

In Pipe Dreams, a Smiler will chase you through a dark hallway, requiring a continuous sprint to the exit. The Electrical Station introduces **Hounds**, which can be stunned with your flashlight. The Terror Hotel is home to both male and female **Death Moths**. Male moths are easily dispatched with bug spray, while female moths require stealth due to their motion sensitivity and the heat they generate.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Level Strategies: Puzzles and Entity Evasion">
Beyond the initial encounters, levels like the Abandoned Office, Level Fun!, and The End demand keen observation and precise execution to overcome their unique puzzles and dangerous inhabitants.

In the Abandoned Office, you'll solve a vending machine puzzle by counting furniture items and then navigate a camera-filled area by sneaking past detection zones. **Windows** entities appear outside, but are harmless if you keep your distance.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Office Puzzle">
    Count furniture items (chairs, desks, computers) in specific rooms. Input these counts into corresponding vending machines to unlock a door.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Camera Evasion">
    Carefully observe camera patrol patterns. Move between their blind spots. If detected, you'll restart the section but can use a vent shortcut.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Terror Hotel Key Collection">
    In the Lobby, solve the painting age puzzle. Use bug spray on male Death Moths to collect Moth Jelly, then feed it to the dumbwaiter for three keys.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Level Fun! is infested with **Partygoers**. These creatures are both visual and auditory, requiring stealth and quick hiding. If they aggro, quickly find a table to hide under. The "Level The End" features the blind **Crawler** entity, which relies on sound. Collecting tapes while distracting the Crawler with fire alarms is key.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Endgame Levels and Multiple Endings">
The final stages of Escape the Backrooms lead through challenging environments and offer multiple pathways to escape, each with its own ending. From the treacherous Level 94 to the intricate puzzles of Level Fun+, your ultimate goal is to uncover the true ending.

Level 94, "The Village," features a day-night cycle. During the day, run towards the distant castle. At night, hide in houses, vans, or closets to avoid **Animations**. The castle itself involves a block puzzle and a survival encounter with the **Animated King**, who must be constantly watched to avoid a lethal attack.

| Ending Path     | Key Levels Involved            | Unique Challenge                      | Entities Present          |
|-----------------|--------------------------------|---------------------------------------|---------------------------|
| Splat Ending (A)| Level 55.1                     | Photo collection, elevator descent    | Bacteria, Smilers, Skin Stealers |
| All Alone (B)   | Overgrowth, Grassrooms         | Survivor sacrifice, parkour           | Bacteria, Bone Thief      |
| I'm Home (C)    | Level 1, Level 922             | Hub door access, staircase jump       | Smilers                   |
| Kitty's House (D)| Level 7, Level 974             | Toy collection, Kitty's gaze          | Kitty                     |
| True Ending     | All alternate endings unlocked | Arcade game interaction (Level 3999)| None                      |

<Callout type="info">
Unlocking the true ending requires completing all four alternate endings first. Each ending branches off from different levels or the M.E.G. Base.
</Callout>

Level Fun+ presents four distinct challenges to collect keys: a Simon Says balloon game, blinding **Party Hosts** with fireworks, illuminating symbols with glow sticks while avoiding a blind **Partygoer**, and popping balloons. Each challenge tests different skills, from memory to precise timing.

The "Death Slides" in Overgrowth involve a grim task: sacrificing injured survivors to deactivate the slides, all while evading multiple **Bacteria**. The Grassrooms demand constant movement on the grass to avoid death, leading to a parkour section to find an exit.

Understanding the specific behaviors and weaknesses of each entity is the cornerstone of survival in Escape the Backrooms. This section details how to counter the most dangerous inhabitants.

| **Hounds**       | Electrical Station, Terror Hotel | Aggressive, chases on sight   | Stun with flashlight (multiple times), then run |
| **Female Death Moths**| Terror Hotel, Level 8 | Motion sensitive, high temp. | Crouch, sneak, run if spotted, use steam valves |

Flashlights are invaluable for stunning Hounds and revealing hidden paths in "Lights Out." Always ensure your battery is charged if possible.

In multiplayer, use the ID feature to distinguish them from real players. **Wretches** and the **Crawler** are blind, making stealth and sound management crucial for evasion. The **Animated King** requires constant visual contact to prevent attacks, while **Animations** on Level 94 necessitate finding safe indoor hiding spots during the night cycle.
