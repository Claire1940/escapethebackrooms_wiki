### Common Reasons for Voice Chat Failure

Several factors can contribute to your mic not working in Escape the Backrooms. Understanding these can help you pinpoint the exact issue.

| Potential Cause           | Description                                                                 | Impact on Game                                  |
| :------------------------ | :-------------------------------------------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------- |
| **Incorrect Input Device** | Windows or Steam selects the wrong microphone (e.g., webcam mic instead of headset). | No audio input detected from desired mic.       |
| **Muted Microphone**       | Microphone is muted in Windows, Steam, or physically.                       | Voice chat completely disabled.                 |
| **Privacy Settings**       | Windows privacy settings block apps from accessing the microphone.          | Game cannot access microphone hardware.         |
| **Outdated Drivers**       | Audio drivers are old or corrupted, leading to input issues.                | Inconsistent or non-existent microphone function. |
| **Antivirus/Firewall**     | Security software interferes with game's access to hardware or network.     | Blocks voice data transmission.                 |
| **Steam Voice Settings**   | Steam's specific voice settings are misconfigured (e.g., default input).    | Game receives no input from Steam's audio channel. |

<Section icon={Target} title="Configuring Windows and Steam for Voice Chat">
Once you've identified potential issues, the next step is to systematically adjust your system and Steam settings to ensure your microphone is properly detected and utilized by Escape the Backrooms. This involves a few key steps to make sure your preferred microphone is the active input device.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Verify Windows Sound Input">
    Go to **Settings** > **System** > **Sound**. Under the "Input" section, select the microphone you intend to use for the game. Ensure there is movement on the input level indicator when you speak, and that the microphone is not muted.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Adjust Steam Voice Settings">
    Open your Steam client. Navigate to **Steam** (top-left) > **Settings** > **Voice**. In the "Voice Input Device" dropdown, explicitly select your desired microphone instead of "Default." You can also use the "Start Microphone Test" to confirm it's working.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Disable Other Microphones">
    In Windows Sound Settings, under the "Recording" tab (accessible via "More sound settings" or "Sound Control Panel"), right-click and disable any other microphones you are not actively using. This prevents conflicts and ensures the game defaults to your primary device.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Check Windows Privacy Settings">
    Go to **Settings** > **Privacy & security** > **Microphone**. Ensure "Microphone access" is turned on, and that "Let desktop apps access your microphone" is also enabled. Scroll down to find "Escape the Backrooms" and ensure its access is toggled on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Potential Conflicts">
Having multiple active microphone devices can confuse the system and the game. Disabling unused mics is a critical step to ensure your primary device functions correctly.
</Callout>

If the standard troubleshooting steps haven't resolved your **Escape the Backrooms voice chat not working** problem, it's time to delve into more advanced solutions. These methods address less common but equally impactful issues, such as specific game settings or software conflicts.

One often overlooked solution involves using the Windows Volume Mixer while the game is running. This allows you to specifically assign your microphone to the game's audio input.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Access Volume Mixer">
    Launch **Escape the Backrooms** and then minimize it. Right-click the speaker icon in your system tray and select "Open Sound settings" or "Sound Control Panel". Navigate to "Sound" and then "Volume mixer."
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Configure Game Input">
    In the Volume Mixer, locate **Escape the Backrooms**. Ensure the input device for the game is explicitly set to your desired headset microphone or equivalent, not "Default."
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Restart the Game">
    After making any changes, always restart Escape the Backrooms to ensure the new settings are applied correctly.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Antivirus and Firewall Check">
Sometimes, security software can mistakenly block game processes from accessing your microphone. Temporarily disable your antivirus or add Escape the Backrooms to its exception list to see if this resolves the issue. Remember to re-enable it afterward.
</Callout>
